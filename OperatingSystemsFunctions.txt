When using macOS, the operating system can carry out services and functions of applications.  The macOS monitors what is needed to run specific applications on the machine.  The OS tells the computer what to do essentially. 
The macOS operating system acts as a communicator.  It interacts with the computer hardware giving the computer commands that will hopefully produce results.  The OS uses language that the computer can understand and interpret, producing specific functions. 
Operating systems such as macOS act as the manager of the computer. One feature of macOS is utilizing Mission Control to tell you what is actually taking place "behind the scenes." Mission Control gives you a quick picture of what is running on your machine. 
One great feature of the macOS is that there is a huge selection of apps that can be used using the Cloud. There is incredible versatility in the devices, making it so user friendly. You can utilize many apps on a variety of devices, not just your desktop or laptop.
